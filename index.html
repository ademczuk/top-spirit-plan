How I work06 / 16
AI training rollout · 1 of 3

AI training in three phases

Phase 1 · Foundations
Ground rules first
Together we set the rules: what may go into AI tools, what never does. A short survey shows where each team stands. No test, no pressure.
Phase 2 · Training
Small groups, real tasks
Office and order desk, sales, finance, logistics, leadership. Each group practices on its own weekly work, not on toy examples.
Phase 3 · Making it stick
Part of normal work
One go-to colleague per department, a weekly open hour for questions, results shared with everyone once a month.

The three phases run inside the same first 90 days as the rest of the plan. The weekly open hour then continues as a standing routine.

AI training rollout07 / 16
AI training rollout · 2 of 3

What a training session looks like

  • 90 minutes, a group of four to six colleagues
  • Everyone brings one real task from their own week
  • A supplier email, a price list, a monthly report: we do it together with the AI tool
  • Every session ends with each person naming one task they now do faster

No classroom theory. The training happens on the work itself, so the time is repaid the same week.

Proof, not promises14 / 16
Fair questions

Questions you may already have

Will AI replace people here?
The approval rule keeps every decision and every customer relationship with your people. The aim is to shrink repetitive typing and checking. What you do with the freed hours is your call, not mine.
Is our data safe?
Customer and pricing data only enters tools your IT has approved. Nothing goes into public AI tools. Written rules say what may enter which tool, agreed with your IT in week one.
Why not wait for the new ERP?
Waiting means months more of the same manual work. Everything here runs on export files and agreed definitions, so it moves with you into whichever ERP you choose, and it makes that choice easier.
What if you leave?
Everything stays with Top Spirit: the process maps, the glossary, the dashboards, and a trained go-to colleague in each department. It is built so your team can run it without me.
